# Mesuring light basics

One advantage of using phyiscal light units is that we have ability validate our equation.

When we are talking about meassuring **Luminace** we are measuring candela per meter squared which intersects on a plane with a stridian (which is angle measured in 3D) of 5 degree in picture below.

![[measuring-tool-for-lumance-2.png]]

So with this we can work out the intensity of light when using the inverse of the inverse square law of light, if we know the difference.
$$I = E \cdot d^2$$

This might not be as important as just matching the real to get nice accurate lighthing.
